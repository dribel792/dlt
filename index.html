Fund Flow Mechanism
Nexo · Tangany · DLT

Describes how client collateral moves between Nexo (lending platform), Tangany (qualified custodian), and DLT (derivatives platform) — covering both the original pledge model and the revised Nexo-centric snapshot model.

Original Model
Pledge & Repledge — No Physical Fund Movement

In this model, used margin is pledged by the user to DLT, which repledges it back to Nexo. Collateral stays at Tangany throughout — no physical movement between Nexo, Tangany, or DLT for the pledged margin. Net PnL and funding rates settle T+1 between Nexo and Tangany (DLT is the venue computing PnL; Tangany is the custodian holding the cash).

1
User holds funds in Nexo Lending

Client's USDC is sitting in Nexo's lending product, earning yield. Not yet allocated to derivatives.

2
User requests transfer to derivatives account

User instructs Nexo to move 5,000 USDC to their derivatives sub-account.

User → Nexo: instruction
3
Physical settlement into Tangany (T+0 to T+3, or weekly)

The 5,000 USDC moves into Tangany as the qualified custodian holding the client's collateral. Settlement can occur T+3 or batched weekly.

Nexo → Tangany: 5,000 USDC (physical settlement)
4
User opens a leveraged position on DLT

User opens a 10,000 USDC notional position at 5× leverage. Initial Margin required = 2,000 USDC (20%).

Total collateral at Tangany: 5,000 USDC | Used margin: 2,000 USDC | Free margin: 3,000 USDC
5
User pledges used margin to DLT

The 2,000 USDC of used (initial) margin is pledged by the user to DLT as collateral for the open position. DLT can only touch this pledged margin — unused collateral (3,000 USDC) is not accessible to DLT.

User → DLT: pledge 2,000 USDC (legal / book-entry, no physical move)
6
DLT repledges margin back to Nexo

DLT repledges the 2,000 USDC to Nexo. This creates a secured lending relationship: Nexo retains economic interest in the collateral while DLT holds the operational pledge.

DLT → Nexo: repledge 2,000 USDC (legal / book-entry)
↻
Daily settlement — PnL & Funding Rates (T+1)

Pledged collateral stays at Tangany — no physical movement. Only the net PnL and funding rate balances across all users settle Nexo ↔ Tangany (cash leg) on a T+1 basis. DLT instructs settlement; Tangany executes the cash transfer.

Nexo ↔ Tangany: net PnL + funding rates (T+1)
Key Mechanics — Original Model
  • DLT access scope: DLT can only touch the used margin (2,000 USDC) pledged to it. The remaining 3,000 USDC free margin at Tangany is off-limits.
  • No physical movement: The pledge and repledge are legal/book-entry arrangements. Funds stay at Tangany throughout.
  • PnL settlement: Net gains and losses + funding rates across all users are cash-settled between Nexo and Tangany on a T+1 basis.
  • Custodian role: Tangany holds physical custody of all client collateral as the qualified custodian. Neither Nexo nor DLT takes direct custody.
Revised Model
Nexo Snapshot-Driven — Funds Move to Nexo When Possible

In this updated model, Nexo takes periodic snapshots of used margin across all users. Instead of a legal pledge, Nexo physically moves funds between Tangany and Nexo based on the delta between snapshots. This maximises capital efficiency on Nexo's side while keeping unused margin safely at Tangany.

1
User holds funds in Nexo Lending

Same starting point — client's USDC is in Nexo's lending product.

2
User requests transfer to derivatives account

User instructs Nexo to move 5,000 USDC to their derivatives sub-account.

User → Nexo: instruction
3
Physical settlement into Tangany (T+0 to T+3, or weekly)

The 5,000 USDC moves into Tangany as the qualified custodian. Settlement window is T+3 or batched weekly.

Nexo → Tangany: 5,000 USDC (physical settlement)
4
User opens a leveraged position on DLT

User opens a 10,000 USDC notional position at 5× leverage. Initial Margin = 2,000 USDC.

DLT records 2,000 USDC as used margin for this user
5
Nexo takes periodic snapshots of total used margin

Nexo snapshots total used margin across all users on a defined cadence (hourly, daily, or weekly). The snapshot captures the aggregate of all open positions across all DLT users.

Snapshot cadence: hourly / daily / weekly (configurable)
6
Nexo moves funds based on snapshot delta

Nexo computes the delta between the current snapshot and the last one:

• If used margin increased (more open positions): Nexo pulls funds from Tangany to Nexo equal to the delta
• If used margin decreased (positions closed): Nexo pushes funds from Nexo back to Tangany equal to the freed delta
• Net zero change: no movement required

Tangany ↔ Nexo: delta amount (physical, batch)
↻
Daily settlement — PnL & Funding Rates (T+1)

Net PnL and funding rates across all users and all trades settle between Nexo and Tangany on a T+1 basis.

Nexo ↔ Tangany: net PnL + funding rates (T+1)
Key Mechanics — Revised Model
  • Snapshot-driven: Nexo aggregates used margin across all users at a configurable cadence (hourly/daily/weekly). No user-level action required.
  • Delta movement only: Only the change in aggregate used margin triggers a physical fund move — not individual position opens/closes.
  • Bidirectional: When positions are opened, funds move Tangany→Nexo. When positions close, freed margin returns Nexo→Tangany.
  • Unused margin stays at Tangany: DLT's constraint is preserved — DLT can only touch actively-used margin, not free client collateral.
  • PnL settlement: T+1 net PnL + funding rate settlement runs between Nexo and Tangany.

Ideal Model ⚠ Regulatory Risk
No Funds Leave Nexo Lending — Full In-House Custody

⚠ Regulatory Concern

If funds never leave Nexo Lending, Nexo effectively holds and controls the derivatives collateral — potentially putting Nexo in the role of collateral custodian for the derivatives product. This may require Nexo to hold additional regulatory licences (e.g. qualified custodian status for derivatives collateral) and could conflict with Tangany's role. Legal and compliance review required before implementation.

Nexo's ideal outcome: client funds stay within Nexo Lending throughout the lifecycle. Tangany is bypassed entirely. The derivatives account is a ledger sub-account within Nexo, not a separate custodial wallet.

1
User holds funds in Nexo Lending

Client's 5,000 USDC is in Nexo's lending product, earning yield. This is the starting state.

2
User requests allocation to derivatives account

User requests Nexo to move 5,000 USDC to their derivatives sub-account. Nexo performs an internal ledger transfer only — no funds leave Nexo's balance sheet.

Nexo internal: re-label 5,000 USDC → derivatives sub-account (book-entry)
✗
Step 3 eliminated — no Tangany movement

Funds do not move to Tangany. Tangany is not involved. Nexo retains physical custody of all client collateral on its own balance sheet.

4
User opens a leveraged position on DLT

User opens a 10,000 USDC notional position at 5× leverage. Initial Margin = 2,000 USDC. Nexo's internal system marks 2,000 USDC as encumbered / used margin.

Nexo internal: 2,000 USDC flagged as used margin | 3,000 USDC remains free
↻
PnL & Funding Rate settlement (T+1)

Net PnL and funding rates across all users settle between Nexo and Tangany on T+1 — same as all other models.

Nexo ↔ Tangany: net PnL + funding rates (T+1)
⚠ Why This Likely Doesn't Work Regulatorily
  • Collateral custody creep: By holding and controlling derivatives margin on its own balance sheet, Nexo assumes the legal role of collateral custodian — a function typically requiring separate regulatory approval (e.g. qualified custodian licence under MiFID II, EMIR, or equivalent).
  • Conflict of interest: Nexo is simultaneously the lender (Nexo Lending) and the collateral holder for a derivatives venue — a structural conflict regulators are sensitive to.
  • Commingling risk: Client derivatives collateral sitting inside Nexo's lending book creates commingling exposure — regulators typically require segregation.
  • Tangany's role: Tangany exists precisely to provide that third-party qualified custodian buffer. Removing Tangany removes the structural separation that makes the original model regulatorily defensible.
  • Recommendation: Treat this model as a theoretical optimum that requires a full legal and compliance review before any discussion with Nexo's regulatory team.
